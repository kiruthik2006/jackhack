Women's Legal Rights

Comprehensive legal protections designed to safeguard women's rights across various domains including workplace, home, and public spaces.

Protection from Violence

Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

  • • Civil law remedy for domestic violence victims
  • • Protection orders to restrain abusers
  • • Right to reside in shared household
  • • Monetary relief and compensation
  • • Custody orders for children

Indian Penal Code Sections

  • • Section 354: Assault to outrage modesty
  • • Section 354A-D: Sexual harassment, voyeurism, stalking
  • • Section 376: Rape and punishment
  • • Section 498A: Cruelty by husband or relatives

Workplace Rights

Sexual Harassment at Workplace (POSH) Act, 2013

  • • Mandatory Internal Complaints Committee
  • • Time-bound inquiry (90 days)
  • • Confidentiality of proceedings
  • • Protection against victimization

Maternity Benefit Act, 1961

  • • 26 weeks paid maternity leave
  • • Work from home options
  • • Crèche facilities mandatory
  • • Protection from dismissal during pregnancy

Equal Remuneration Act, 1976

  • • Equal pay for equal work
  • • No discrimination in recruitment
  • • Prevention of gender-based pay discrimination

Marriage & Family Rights

  • • Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961
  • • Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006
  • • Hindu Succession Act, 1956 (equal inheritance)
  • • Special Marriage Act, 1954

Health & Reproductive Rights

  • • Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971
  • • Pre-conception Diagnostic Techniques Act, 1994
  • • Surrogacy Regulation Act, 2021
  • • Right to safe and legal abortion

Property & Succession Rights

  • • Hindu Succession Act, 1956 (amended 2005)
  • • Daughter as coparcener in ancestral property
  • • Equal inheritance rights
  • • Property rights for married women

Transgender Legal Rights

Comprehensive legal framework ensuring dignity, equality, and protection for transgender individuals in India.

Identity & Recognition

Transgender Persons Act, 2019

  • • Right to self-perceived gender identity
  • • Protection from discrimination
  • • Certificate of identity issuance
  • • No medical surgery required for certification

NALSA Judgment, 2014

  • • Legal recognition as "third gender"
  • • Access to reservations in education and employment
  • • Healthcare facilities and separate HIV surveillance
  • • Right to self-identification

Protection & Welfare

Constitutional Protections

  • • Article 14: Right to equality
  • • Article 15: Prohibition of discrimination
  • • Article 16: Equality in public employment
  • • Article 21: Right to life and personal liberty

Welfare Measures

  • • Scholarships for transgender students
  • • Skill development programs
  • • Health insurance schemes
  • • Garima Greh (shelter homes)

Education Rights

  • • Inclusive education policies
  • • Reservation in educational institutions
  • • Protection from discrimination in schools
  • • Scholarship programs

Healthcare Rights

  • • Access to gender-affirming healthcare
  • • Separate wards in hospitals
  • • Mental health support
  • • HIV/AIDS prevention and care

Employment Rights

  • • Reservation in public employment
  • • Protection from workplace discrimination
  • • Equal opportunity in career growth
  • • Vocational training programs

Landmark Legal Milestones

Key judicial pronouncements and legislative developments that shaped gender justice in India.

2014

NALSA v. Union of India

Supreme Court legally recognized transgender persons as "third gender" and affirmed their fundamental rights.

2013

POSH Act

Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act provided comprehensive protection against workplace harassment.

2018

Navtej Singh Johar v. Union of India

Decriminalized consensual same-sex relationships by reading down Section 377 of IPC.

2019

Transgender Persons Act

Comprehensive legislation for protection of transgender rights and welfare.

2005

Domestic Violence Act

Provided civil remedies for victims of domestic violence with wide-ranging protections.
